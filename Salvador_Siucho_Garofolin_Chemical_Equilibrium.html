⚖️ Equilibrium Constant (K)

A quantitative measure of the extent to which a reaction proceeds toward products at equilibrium.

  • K > 1: Products favored
  • K < 1: Reactants favored
  • K = 1: Comparable amounts
  • Temperature dependent only
🔄

Le Chatelier's Principle

"If a dynamic equilibrium is disturbed by changing the conditions, the position of equilibrium moves to counteract the disturbance and restore a new equilibrium."

The System Always Fights Back!

📝 Writing K Expression

For a general reaction: aA + bB ⇌ cC + dD

K = [C]ᶜ[D]ᵈ / [A]ᵃ[B]ᵇ
  • Products in numerator
  • Reactants in denominator
  • Coefficients become exponents
  • Solids & pure liquids are omitted!

🔍 Reaction Quotient (Q)

Used to determine the direction a reaction will shift to reach equilibrium:

Q = [Products] / [Reactants]
  • Q < K: Shift → Right (toward products)
  • Q > K: Shift → Left (toward reactants)
  • Q = K: At equilibrium
← System responds to stress →

📊 Kp and Kc

Two ways to express equilibrium:

  • Kc: Concentration (mol/L)
  • Kp: Partial pressure (atm)
  • Relationship: Kp = Kc(RT)^Δn
  • Δn = moles gas products − moles gas reactants

📐 ICE Tables

A systematic method to calculate equilibrium concentrations:

  • Initial concentration
  • Change (use −x and +x)
  • Equilibrium expression
  • Solve for x, then find all concentrations

🧱 Heterogeneous Equilibria

When reactants and products are in different phases:

  • Pure solids are NOT included in K
  • Pure liquids are NOT included in K
  • Only aqueous & gas species appear
  • Example: CaCO₃(s) ⇌ CaO(s) + CO₂(g) → K = [CO₂]

⚡ Effects of Changes on Equilibrium (Le Chatelier's Principle)

💧

Concentration

Increase [reactant] → shifts right
Increase [product] → shifts left
Decrease → opposite shift

🌡️

Temperature

Exothermic (ΔH < 0): Heat = product
↑T → shifts left
Endothermic: ↑T → shifts right

💨

Pressure

↑ Pressure → shifts toward fewer gas moles
↓ Pressure → shifts toward more gas moles

📦

Volume

↓ Volume = ↑ Pressure
Shifts toward side with fewer moles of gas to relieve stress

⚡

Catalyst

NO effect on equilibrium position!
Speeds up both forward and reverse reactions equally
System reaches equilibrium faster

🎯 Key Takeaway

Chemical equilibrium is a dynamic balance where forward and reverse reaction rates are equal. The equilibrium constant K tells us the ratio of products to reactants, while Le Chatelier's Principle predicts how the system responds to external stresses—always shifting to counteract the change and restore balance.
